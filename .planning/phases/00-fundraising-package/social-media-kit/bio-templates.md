## LinkedIn Company Description (2000 character max)

```
Takes a Village is a nonprofit platform bringing radical transparency to substance abuse treatment.

The substance abuse treatment industry in the United States is a $42 billion sector with no standardized review system, no verified outcome data, and limited cost transparency. Families in crisis make life-changing decisions based on marketing claims and unverified reviews. The FTC has documented predatory patient brokering practices. SAMHSA's treatment locator -- the government baseline -- lists ~17,000 facilities but includes no reviews, no cost data, and no outcome tracking.

We are building a platform where:
- Every review is verified (reviewer attendance confirmed) but displayed anonymously
- Facilities cannot pay to rank or suppress negative reviews
- Transparency scores surface how honest and complete a facility's information is
- A funding finder matches people to grants and scholarships they qualify for
- AI agents meet people where they are -- on social platforms -- with answers grounded in verified data

Our technology approach is intentionally different. We use the OriginTrail Decentralized Knowledge Graph (DKG) so that facility data, outcomes, and reviews live on infrastructure no single party controls. Identity is handled through Polkadot Proof of Personhood -- enabling verified but anonymous participation. Platform governance is through a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization), giving the community ownership of the platform's direction.

Before writing a line of code, we mapped three federal compliance gates: Anti-Kickback Statute review for our incentive mechanism, 42 CFR Part 2 (healthcare privacy) review for our verification flow, and FinCEN review for our funding disbursement structure. We believe legal diligence is a feature, not an obstacle.

We are US-first with a global architecture. Currently pre-funding and building in public.

Website: https://takesavillage.health
GitHub: https://github.com/paxtontribe/TakesaVillage

Need help now? Call 1-800-662-4357 (SAMHSA National Helpline -- free, confidential, 24/7)
```
