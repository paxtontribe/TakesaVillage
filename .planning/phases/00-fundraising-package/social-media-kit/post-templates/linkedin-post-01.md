## Post

Why I'm building a nonprofit treatment transparency platform.

The substance abuse treatment industry in the United States is a $42 billion sector. There is no standardized, verified review system for any of the ~17,000 facilities listed in the federal government's treatment locator.

Think about that for a moment. When someone you love needs help, you're expected to make a life-changing, $15,000-$27,000 decision based on marketing claims, unverified Google reviews, and directories that may route you to whichever facility pays the highest referral fee.

The FTC has documented predatory patient brokering. The Eliminating Kickbacks in Recovery Act (EKRA) of 2018 was passed specifically because the problem was so severe. And yet, the information infrastructure has not changed. SAMHSA's treatment locator -- the best free tool available -- has no reviews, no cost data, and no outcome tracking.

Takes a Village is a nonprofit platform designed to change this:

-- Every review is verified (the reviewer actually attended the facility) but displayed anonymously (stigma protection is critical in substance use recovery)
-- Facilities cannot pay to rank or suppress negative reviews
-- Transparency scores surface how honest and complete a facility's information actually is
-- A funding finder matches people to grants and scholarships they qualify for
-- AI matching helps people find the right level of care, grounded in verified data

We're building on the OriginTrail Decentralized Knowledge Graph (DKG) so that treatment data lives on infrastructure no single company controls. Identity uses Polkadot Proof of Personhood for verified but anonymous participation. Governance is through a DAO -- community-owned accountability.

Before writing a single line of code, we mapped three federal compliance gates: the Anti-Kickback Statute for our incentive mechanism, 42 CFR Part 2 (stricter than HIPAA) for our verification flow, and FinCEN regulations for our funding disbursement structure. We believe legal diligence is a signal of seriousness, not a barrier to innovation.

We are pre-launch, building in public, and looking for:

-- Healthcare professionals who see the gap in treatment information infrastructure
-- Nonprofit leaders with experience navigating SAMHSA, state licensing, and federal compliance
