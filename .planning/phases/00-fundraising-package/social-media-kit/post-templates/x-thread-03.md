## Tweet 2/5

The core problem in treatment data is trust.

Facilities self-report claims. Directories accept payment to rank results. Reviews are unverified and suppressible. When data lives on centralized infrastructure, the entity that controls it can manipulate it.

We needed a different foundation.

---

## Tweet 3/5

@OriginTrail's DKG lets us publish facility profiles, reviews, and outcomes as Knowledge Assets -- structured, verifiable, and stored on decentralized infrastructure.

No single party can quietly edit or delete data. Not us, not the facility, not a commercial partner.

That's the trust primitive we needed.

---

## Tweet 4/5

Combined with @Polkadot Proof of Personhood for identity:

- Reviewers are verified humans who actually attended a facility
- Reviews are displayed anonymously (stigma protection is critical in substance use)
- One person, one review per facility per period (no Sybil attacks)

Authenticated-anonymous. The pattern that makes reviews trustworthy.
