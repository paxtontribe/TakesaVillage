## Slide 1: The $42 Billion Industry

**Headline:** The US substance abuse treatment industry is worth $42 billion.
**Body:** That's billion with a B. And there is no standardized system for verifying whether a facility actually delivers quality care.
**Design:** Large "$42B" in amber (#F4A261), white supporting text on teal

---

## Slide 2: No Verified Reviews

**Headline:** There is no verified review system for treatment facilities.
**Body:** Anyone can post a review on Google or Yelp -- staff, competitors, people who never set foot in the building. Facilities routinely pay to suppress negative reviews.
**Design:** Star rating graphic with question mark, white text on teal

---

## Slide 3: FTC Brokering Cases

**Headline:** The FTC has documented predatory patient brokering.
**Body:** Some "treatment directories" route people to whichever facility pays the highest referral fee -- not the best fit. Referral fees can reach $10,000 per admission.
**Design:** Dollar sign graphic, white text on teal

---

## Slide 4: SAMHSA's Limitations

**Headline:** The government's treatment locator lists 17,000 facilities -- with no quality data.
**Body:** SAMHSA's locator is the best free tool available. But it has no reviews, no cost data, no outcome tracking, and frequently lists closed or moved facilities.
**Design:** Map pin graphic with question mark, white text on teal
