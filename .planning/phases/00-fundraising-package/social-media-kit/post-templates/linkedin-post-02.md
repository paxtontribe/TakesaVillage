## Post

The $42 billion substance abuse treatment industry has no verified review system. Here's what we're doing about it.

The numbers:
-- 48.7 million Americans had a substance use disorder in 2023
-- Only 1 in 4 received any treatment
-- ~17,000 treatment facilities are listed in the US federal locator
-- Zero have a system for verified, authenticated reviews
-- Average residential treatment costs $15,000-$27,000 for 30 days
-- Most facilities do not publish actual costs

The gap:
SAMHSA's treatment locator is the most comprehensive public resource. It includes facility names, addresses, treatment types, and accepted payment types. What it does not include: reviews from actual patients, cost data, outcome tracking, or quality metrics of any kind.

The result is that people in crisis -- or their families -- make one of the most important and expensive decisions of their lives with almost no trustworthy information.

What we're building:
Takes a Village is a nonprofit platform designed specifically for this gap.

1. Verified reviews -- Every reviewer's attendance is confirmed. Reviews are displayed anonymously because stigma in substance use recovery is real and dangerous.
2. Transparency scores -- Facilities earn visibility through completeness and honesty, not marketing spend. No pay-to-rank. No referral fees.
3. Funding navigation -- A searchable directory of grants, scholarships, and sliding-scale programs with eligibility matching.
4. AI matching -- Describe your situation, get matched to the right level of care and facilities that fit.

All of this built on decentralized infrastructure (OriginTrail DKG) so that data cannot be quietly edited, suppressed, or manipulated by any single party.

We're pre-launch and building in public. If this problem resonates with you, join the waitlist or reach out.
