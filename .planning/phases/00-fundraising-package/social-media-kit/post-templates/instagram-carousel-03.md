## Slide 1: The Scale

**Headline:** 48.7 million Americans had a substance use disorder in 2023.
**Body:** That's roughly 1 in 6 adults. Substance use disorder affects every community, every demographic, every income level.
**Design:** Large "48.7M" in amber (#F4A261), white supporting text on teal

---

## Slide 2: The Treatment Gap

**Headline:** Only 1 in 4 people with a substance use disorder receive any treatment.
**Body:** Cost, stigma, availability, and a lack of trustworthy information keep millions of people from getting help.
**Design:** "1 in 4" large text, pie chart visual, white text on teal

---

## Slide 3: The System

**Headline:** ~17,000 treatment facilities are listed in the US. Zero have verified reviews.
**Body:** SAMHSA's locator is the most comprehensive public database. It has no reviews, no cost data, no outcome tracking, and frequently lists facilities that have closed or moved.
**Design:** "17K facilities / 0 verified reviews" in contrasting text, white on teal

---

## Slide 4: The Cost

**Headline:** Average residential treatment costs $15,000-$27,000 for 30 days.
**Body:** Most facilities do not publish their actual costs. Insurance coverage varies wildly. People in crisis make $15K+ decisions with almost no reliable information.
**Design:** Dollar signs at different sizes, white text on teal
