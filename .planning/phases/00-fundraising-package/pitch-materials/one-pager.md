## The Problem

The US substance abuse treatment industry is a $42 billion market serving ~17,000 facilities -- with no verified review system for any of them. SAMHSA's national locator lacks reviews, cost data, and outcome tracking. Commercial directories are referral-fee machines that route patients based on who pays the most, not who provides the best care. Families in crisis are forced to make life-or-death decisions with unreliable information.

## The Solution

- **Verified Reviews:** Authenticated-anonymous reviews from people who actually attended treatment -- identity verified, display fully anonymous, non-suppressible
- **Transparency Scores:** Merit-based facility scoring on verification completeness, outcomes disclosure, and review history -- no facility pays to rank
- **Decentralized Data:** All facility data published to a knowledge graph no single party controls, making every claim traceable and auditable
- **AI-Powered Discovery:** Natural language matching to appropriate care level and facilities, sourced from verified data

## How It Works

Takes a Village starts with SAMHSA's national dataset of ~17,000 US facilities as a baseline, then layers on verified facility-submitted profiles, authenticated reviews, and funding information. All data lives on the OriginTrail Decentralized Knowledge Graph -- a shared database where no single company owns or controls the data. User identity is verified through Polkadot Proof of Personhood, which confirms each person is a unique, real human without exposing any personal information. AI agents on social platforms can answer treatment questions in real time, citing verified data instead of making things up.
