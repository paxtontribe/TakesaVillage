# Investor Targets -- Takes a Village

**Last updated:** 2026-03-29

---

> **Important: Takes a Village is a nonprofit. There is no equity, no token sale, and no investor return in the traditional sense. The "investment" targets below are impact investors, grant co-funders, and ecosystem participants who fund social good infrastructure. Every conversation with a potential funder must make this clear upfront.**

---

## Category 1: Impact Investors / Impact Funds Focused on Health Equity

**What they fund:** Organizations creating measurable social impact in healthcare, particularly for underserved populations.

**Why TaV fits:** Directly addresses information asymmetry in substance abuse treatment -- a $42B industry where vulnerable populations are systematically harmed by predatory referral practices. Nonprofit model ensures alignment with impact mandate.

**Specific targets to research:**
- **Blue Meridian Partners** -- Health equity focus, large-scale nonprofit investments
- **RWJF Impact Investments** -- Robert Wood Johnson Foundation's impact investing arm (separate from their grants program)
- **Social Finance** -- Pay-for-success models applied to substance use disorder outcomes
- **Tipping Point Community** -- Bay Area health equity focus
- **Echoing Green** -- Early-stage social enterprise funding (fellowship model)

**Outreach channel:** LinkedIn introductions, healthcare nonprofit conferences, warm introductions from RWJF grant officers
**Pitch angle:** Emphasize slides 2 (Problem), 3 (Who Is Hurt), 9 (Market/Impact), 10 (Legal Awareness). Lead with impact metrics and population served. De-emphasize blockchain terminology.

---

## Category 2: Health Tech Angel Investors / Seed Funds

**What they fund:** Technology companies addressing healthcare inefficiency, access, or transparency.

**Why TaV fits:** Addresses the most opaque segment of healthcare with a technology-first approach. Competitive moat is the authenticated-anonymous review system -- no one else has it.

**Specific targets to research:**
- **Rock Health** -- Digital health venture fund; look for their angel syndicate or nonprofit partnership track
- **General Catalyst (Health Assurance)** -- Hemant Taneja's health transformation thesis; nonprofit alignment is unusual but mission overlap is strong
- **Andreessen Horowitz Bio + Health Fund** -- Large fund with health tech focus; explore their nonprofit advisory relationships
- **Health tech angel syndicates** on AngelList -- search for angels with substance abuse, behavioral health, or health equity keywords
- **Individual angels** with personal connection to addiction/recovery -- often the most motivated funders for this specific mission

**Outreach channel:** AngelList, health tech conferences (HLTH, HIMSS), cold email with one-pager attached, LinkedIn
**Pitch angle:** Emphasize slides 9 (Market -- $42B), 4 (Solution), 8 (Team). Frame the competitive gap: no verified review system exists for 17,000 US treatment facilities. Be upfront about nonprofit status -- the pitch is impact investing, not equity returns.

**Key caveat:** Most health tech funds expect equity returns. TaV as a nonprofit does not fit traditional VC. Target the impact investing arms of these funds, individual angels with personal mission alignment, or explore fiscal sponsorship models that allow tax-deductible donations.

## Category 3: Web3/Crypto Funds With Social Good Mandate

**What they fund:** Projects that demonstrate blockchain infrastructure creating real-world value beyond DeFi and speculation.

**Why TaV fits:** A genuine social good application of Polkadot identity (PoP), OriginTrail DKG, and NeuroWeb AI inference. Demonstrates that Web3 infrastructure can serve people in crisis, not just traders.

**Specific targets to research:**
- **Polkadot Treasury** (covered in grant-targets.md -- the primary Web3 funding path)
- **OriginTrail ecosystem** (covered in grant-targets.md -- relationship-driven partnership)
- **Gitcoin Grants** -- Quadratic funding rounds for public goods; TaV as a "public good healthcare data layer" fits the narrative
- **Protocol Labs / Filecoin Foundation** -- Decentralized data infrastructure focus; TaV's DKG use case aligns with their thesis
- **Ethereum Foundation ESP (Ecosystem Support Program)** -- Funds public goods; TaV uses Base L2 for funding pools

**Outreach channel:** Discord communities, Web3 conferences (Polkadot Decoded, OriginTrail community events, ETHDenver), Twitter/X engagement with ecosystem builders
**Pitch angle:** Emphasize slides 5 (How It Works), 6 (Why Now), 10 (Legal Awareness). Lead with ecosystem value: Polkadot PoP serving real humans in crisis situations. Differentiate from token projects -- TaV is infrastructure, not speculation.

## Category 4: DAOs With Healthcare or Public Goods Focus

**What they fund:** Community-governed treasuries that allocate funds to projects serving the public good or specific mission areas.

**Why TaV fits:** DAO governance is built into the TaV roadmap (Phase 4). The project demonstrates DAO principles applied to healthcare accountability. Natural alignment with public goods funding DAOs.

**Specific targets to research:**
- **Gitcoin DAO** -- Public goods funding; apply through Gitcoin Grants rounds
- **Nouns DAO** -- Funds public goods projects; proposals accepted from anyone; high-visibility if approved
- **MolochDAO** -- Grants for Ethereum ecosystem public goods; TaV uses Base L2
- **Dream DAO** -- Gen Z civic participation; substance abuse transparency resonates with younger demographics
- **UkraineDAO / charitable DAOs** -- Precedent for DAOs funding real-world humanitarian work; look for health-focused equivalents

**Outreach channel:** DAO governance forums, Snapshot proposals, Discord communities, Twitter/X
**Pitch angle:** Frame TaV as a public good: decentralized healthcare data infrastructure that no single company controls. The authenticated-anonymous review system is a novel governance pattern that DAO communities find compelling.
